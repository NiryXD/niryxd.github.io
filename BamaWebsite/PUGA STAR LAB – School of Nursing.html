We are dedicated to studying stress, resilience, and the psychological well-being of older adults living with serious illness and their care partners.

The Stress Aging and Resilience (StAR) Research Lab is dedicated to improving the health and well-being of older adults living with serious illnesses and their family and friends who care for them. Living with serious illness, such as dementia or cancer, can be stressful, and this stress can affect one’s mental health, physical health, and overall quality of life. It can also shape the care that individuals receive. Our research focuses on understanding the daily experiences of older adults and their family caregiver. We study how stress, mood and social interactions unfold in everyday life and how they increase or decrease resilience over time. Our goal is to build the evidence needed to design and implement adaptive, personalized interventions to provide support when and where it is most needed.
